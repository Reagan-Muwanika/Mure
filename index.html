Foundation of Kyambogo University

Kyambogo University is one of public universities in Uganda and a degree awarding institution in the country with its motto "Knowledge and Skills for Service". The university was established in 2003 by merging three intitutions;

  1. Uganda Polytechnic Kyambogo (UPK).
  2. Institution of Teacher Education, Kyambogo (ITEK).
  3. Uganda Nationalstitute of Special Education (UNISE).

Uganda Polytechnic Kyambogo (UPK)

UPK is one of the parts that split off from the Trade and Technical courses at Makerere college
currently Makerere University in 1928. I then became Kampala Technical School.
It moved to Nakawa and became Kampala Technical Institute. In 1958, the school
shifted to Kyambogo hill with the name Uganda Technical College that was later renamed
Uganda Polytechnic Kyambogo (UPK).

Institute of Teacher Education Kyambogo (ITEK)

ITEK started as a government teacher training college in 1948 at Nyakasura in Kabarole district. In 1954,
the school was moved to Kyambogo hill as a national teachers' college and later became ITEK
by statute of parliament in 1989.

Uganda Nationalstitute of Special Education (UNISE)

UNISE was affiliated to the department of Special Education at the faculty of Education at Makerere University,
becoming an autonomous institution by act of parliamentin 1998.

Kyambogo University is lacated at the Kyambogo Hill in Kampala. It is mostly referred to as the Banda based University with over 33,000+ student.
